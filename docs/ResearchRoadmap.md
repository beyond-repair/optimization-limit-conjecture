# Research Roadmap: The Optimization-Limit Conjecture

## Objective

The goal of this research program is to derive the asymptotic obstruction limit $\mathcal{W}$ and determine if it characterizes the empirical value $W^* \approx 0.08$.

## The Three-Level Research Program

### Level 1: Optimization (Foundational)

* **Goal**: Establish the existence of $\mathcal{W}$ as a structural invariant.
* **Method**: Utilize recursive branching graph families to model the conflict between local anchoring ($L_i$) and global recursive consistency ($G$).
* **Milestones**:
  * Completion of Theorem A (Existence of Limit).
    * Verification of Theorem B (Phase Transition to Non-Zero Obstruction).
    * Identification of parameter space $\Theta$ where $\mathcal{W}(\Theta) \approx 0.08$.

### Level 2: Information Theory (Equivalence)

* **Goal**: Demonstrate that $\mathcal{W}$ acts as a universal information-theoretic bottleneck.
* **Method**: Compute the rate-distortion function for the optimized graph family and determine if the "residual tax" $\mathcal{W}$ corresponds to a fundamental compression limit in non-localizable signal structures.

### Level 3: Topology (Geometric Interpretation)

* **Goal**: Interpret $\mathcal{W}$ as a geometric obstruction.
* **Method**: Map the asymptotic residual to the Euler characteristic or similar topological invariants of the "nozzle" structure.
* **Significance**: This links the abstract optimization limit to the physical-phenomenological observations (e.g., lensing/rotation curves) that initiated the study.

---

## Epistemic Standard

This program operates under a strictly hierarchical structure. We do not infer physical properties from the results until Level 3 is satisfied. Each level serves as the necessary logical foundation for the next.
